dkNET-AI · Apache Texera Agent Hackathon

An in-person hackathon hosted by the NIH dkNET project and the Apache Texera team at UC Irvine, May 14–18, 2026.
By Matthew Ball | Wednesday, May 20, 2026

May 14–18, 2026 · UC Irvine

We are thrilled to celebrate the successful conclusion of the recent dkNET-AI · Apache Texera Agent Hackathon! Brought together by the NIH dkNET project and hosted by the Apache Texera team, this intensive, in-person event challenged participants to build the future of human-AI collaborative data science.

Over a dynamic four-day sprint from May 14 to May 18, 2026, at UC Irvine, the event united researchers, students, and data enthusiasts to build intuitive, visual workflows where advanced AI agents co-author data pipelines alongside humans.

01The Hackathon by the Numbers

The dedication and productivity displayed by our community over the weekend were truly remarkable:

23
active participants (combining newcomers and 1-on-1 paired Texera Mentors)
46
hours of non-stop hacking
35
total project submissions generated, with 33 accepted for formal evaluation by our expert judging panel
To supercharge their development, every participant was equipped with a complimentary one-month Claude Code Premium Account ($125 value), integrating cutting-edge AI assistance directly into their command-line interface and IDE environments.

02Final Award Winners & Highlights

Our distinguished panel of judges—Dr. Xi Lu (University at Buffalo), Dr. Zuozhi Wang (Snowflake), and Dr. Yicong Huang (Databricks)—evaluated the submissions based on craft, real workflow leverage, and human-in-the-loop design. Top-performing projects and runners-up were recognized with individual gift card awards ranging from $50 to $100.
